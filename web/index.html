Recognition-gated Workspace Steering

Control the Hidden Workspace in Language Models

A small recognition-driven world model steers a frozen LLM through its global workspace — writes timed by sphurattā events, verified by āgama re-cognition, bounded by autonomy preservation.

By Sharath Sathish · Independent Researcher

Act I

The Uncanny Parallel

What if the functional workspace of an LLM is defined by what it says?

Large language models are black boxes. We feed them tokens; they emit tokens. In between: billions of floating-point operations, millions of parameters, dynamics we barely understand.

But recent work on the J-space — the "global workspace" hypothesis adapted to transformer circuits — has revealed something uncanny. The key insight: verbalizability defines a privileged subspace. The functional core of an LLM is not hidden in some inaccessible layer; it's the activation subspace that the model is *disposed to report*.

This parallels a millennium-old debate in philosophy. The Pratyabhijñā school of Kashmir Śaivism distinguished consciousness (vimarśa, reflexive awareness) from mere luminous manifestation (prakāśa) by exactly this criterion: linguistic reflexivity. What distinguishes conscious grasping from automatic processing is an intrinsically linguistic character — inner speech.

The paper's data contains this split: ~90% of activation variance is non-J-space ("automatic processing") while the small verbalizable component (~6–10% per layer) carries all the flexibly reusable, causally load-bearing function.

Act II

The Instrument: Jacobian-Lens

Reading and writing into the J-space requires a precise tool.

The jacobian-lens is that tool. It provides a read–write interface to the functional core of a frozen LLM:

  • Read: Extract what the workspace currently holds (a sparse basis of verbalizable vectors).
  • Write: Inject sparse non-negative codes over J-lens vectors into the workspace.
  • Measure: Observe uptake (workspace loading, MLP amplification, broadcast-head relay, downstream report).

But the lens alone is an actuator without a control theory. What should be written? When? How do we know it was taken up?

This is where prabodha enters.

Act III

The Band's Voice: Calibration & Legibility

Not every J-space vector is readable. Only the lens trained on a concept carves out a legible subspace.

The first finding (L1–L2): the J-space is carved out by a *band* of layers where verbalizable activations concentrate (~L38–92 in Qwen3-4B). The band is roughly stable across model families and scales.

The second finding (L2b): but here's the kicker — the band's content is **legible only to a band-targeted lens**. A lens trained on a *different* concept sees mostly noise. This means the band encodes concept-specific information, not a generic workspace.

So our controller must first *calibrate* a lens for the target concept on a small corpus (3 seeds, ~500 tokens per seed). The resulting lens defines what we can read and write. It's not universal; it's *articulated* — tuned to the concept-workspace dialogue we're about to establish.

Act IV

Timing & Sphuraṭṭā: The Gating Signal

When to write is as critical as what to write.

In the Pratyabhijñā world model, sphuraṭṭā (often translated "flash" or "vibration") names the moment of recognition — the instant when a previously hidden identity (pratyabhijñā) suddenly *illuminates* consciousness.

For steering, we use sphuraṭṭā as a *gating signal*: write only when entropy is high (the model is uncertain, the workspace is "receptive"). The intuition is simple — if the model is already certain, injecting content is likely noise. If it's uncertain, the write can guide the downstream distribution.

We measure entropy pre-write and apply a threshold (percentile-based). Writes happen at only ~10–30% of decode steps, concentrating steering at the moments of highest uncertainty. The core claim (6-seed confirm tier) shows that entropy-gated writes achieve measurable lift within budget, outperforming rate-matched (equal-write-budget) baselines.

Act V

The Transfer Recipe: Nemotron & Beyond

Does the method generalize? Yes — with calibration.

Steering works on Qwen3-4B with a tuned lens. But can we steer other models?

The transfer recipe (L13–L14): fit a fresh lens on the target model's band using the same calibration corpus (3 seeds, same concept). The lens is cheap — ~30 forward passes, ~2 minutes on a V100. Then apply the same steering logic: entropy-gated writes with the new lens vectors.

Result: on Nemotron-4B-Instruct, the recipe reproduces a measurable lift (4 seeds, confirm tier). The numbers are smaller than Qwen3 (artifact of different lens strength / active range), but the *direction* is consistent. The phenomenon transfers.

Act VI

The Bridge (L20): PWM Integration and Cold-Store Equivalence

What if the controller's write vector came from a learned world model instead of the lens transpose?

The analytic writer (used in L0–L19) writes JTu — the lens transpose applied to a target concept vector. It's interpretable: we control what the workspace receives.

The alternative path: integrate the Pratyabhijñā World Model (PWM), a 6.35M-parameter RSSM trained on language understanding. The PWM has its own memory (CittaStore, a Hopfield associative store) and can produce write vectors via learned retrieval.

L20 Result (now closed): The PWM CittaStore pathway was blocked at dispatch for three loops due to missing stack infrastructure on GB10. When integrated, the frozen (untrained) Hopfield-store recall—initialized with no steering patterns—produces write vectors that steer on all three seeds (42, 123, 777) within the ±0.5-nat entropy budget. Against the analytic JTu baseline:

  • Seed 42: cold store +0.4445 vs analytic +0.4445 (gap = 0.0000, exact match)
  • Seed 123: cold store +0.5556 vs analytic +0.5556 (gap = 0.0000, exact match)
  • Seed 777: cold store +0.4445 vs analytic +0.3334 (gap = +0.1111, cold store favored; = 1/9 quantization unit, within noise)

Honest Framing: The CittaStore is initialized empty — no patterns have been stored or trained. This is not a "trained bridge" but a cold-store proof-of-concept: the PWM write pathway is live and dimensionally compatible with the band-exit Jacobian. A vanilla-initialized Hopfield recall already ≈ the lens-transpose baseline. The real science—does training the store against steering-success targets improve over this cold initialization?—remains open; the infrastructure to test it is now available.

Menu 3 Closure: This was the standing "carried silently" item since menu-driven selection began (L5). Its resolution—cold-recall equivalent to analytic, not better—means the doctrine's steering arm (sphurattā-gated band writes via JTu) remains the canonical finding. The trained-store hypothesis is unconfirmed but executable.

Act VII

Honest Limits: What Didn't Work

Every method has a margin. Here's where the rubber met the road.

Readback (using the model to judge whether a write succeeded) is weak. Balanced accuracy ≈ 0.59 at n=120 — barely above chance. Never use readback as an acceptance gate alone.

Greedy decoding masks decode-time writes; sampling is required. This limits deployment to sampling regimes (most inference).

Amplitude scaling with corpus difficulty is confirmed in direction but fails the strict margin criterion in one hard cell (fail-on-margin). The lift roughly doubles from α=0.1→0.2 across corpora, but one condition is at the margin of significance.

Corpus-amplitude interaction is screened, not confirmed. Multi-arm offsets are unexamined.

All of these are recorded as open items, not silently brushed aside.

Act VIII

Run It Yourself

The method is open. Here's how to steer your own model.

Install the Library

pip install prabodha
prabodha --help  # lens-fit · lens-eval · lens-vis · steer · research

Quickstart: Steer Qwen3-4B

python -c "
from prabodha.steering import fit_lens, steer
import torch

# Fit a lens on a small concept corpus
corpus = ['fire reminds of warmth', 'the flame dances', 'embers glow bright']
lens = fit_lens('Qwen/Qwen3-4B', corpus, concept='fire', num_seeds=3)

# Steer a new prompt through the lens
prompt = 'the fire remembers rivers'
output = steer(prompt, lens, alpha=0.2, method='entropy_gated', budget=500)
print(output)
"

Concepts & Vocabulary

J-space: The verbalizable global workspace of a language model — the subspace of activations disposed to be reported.

Sphuraṭṭā: (Sanskrit: "vibration", "flash") In Pratyabhijñā, the moment of recognition. Here: the gating signal (high entropy → receptive moment).

Vimarśa: (Sanskrit: "reflexive awareness", "inner speech") Linguistic reflexivity; the distinction between conscious (reportable) and automatic processing.

Āgama: (Sanskrit: "testimony", "received knowledge") The LLM's outputs as observations; valid but not supreme — they can be re-cognized by the controller.

Pratyabhijñā: (Sanskrit: "recognition") The philosophy underlying PWM and this work — the doctrine of identity, reciprocal re-cognition, and the unity of knower and known.
