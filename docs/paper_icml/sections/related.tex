\section{Background and Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Global Workspace Theory and the J-Space Result}

Global Workspace Theory holds that cognition is organised around a limited-capacity workspace whose contents are broadcast to, and readable by, many specialist processes \cite{Baars1988,Dehaene1998,Mashour2020,Dehaene2014}. Assessments of the theory's applicability to artificial systems have generally proceeded at the level of architectural checklists \cite{Butlin2023}. The verbalizable-workspace result gave the theory an operational form for transformers: representations that a model can report in words after intervention occupy a small, late-layer subspace (the J-space), identified by the average input-output Jacobian, and this subspace behaves as a functional workspace in the broadcast sense \cite{Anthropic2026Workspace}. The companion Jacobian lens transports a residual vector $h_\ell$ at layer $\ell$ into final-layer coordinates through $J_\ell = \Exp{\partial h_{\mathrm{final}} / \partial h_\ell}$ and decodes it with the model's own unembedding \cite{JacobianLens2026}. The present work takes both the result and the instrument as given, vendors the reference lens implementation unmodified, and asks the successor question: given a readable and writable workspace, what constitutes disciplined use?

\subsection{Activation Steering}

Steering by residual-stream addition has an established literature. Activation addition constructs steering vectors from prompt pairs \cite{Turner2023}; contrastive activation addition extracts directions from labelled behavioural pairs and applies them at a chosen layer \cite{Rimsky2024}; representation engineering generalises the approach with regression and principal-component readouts \cite{Zou2023RepE}; function vectors show that in-context tasks are carried by transportable directions \cite{Todd2024}. All of these methods share mechanics with the present work: a direction, a layer, a coefficient. They differ in what this paper argues are the doctrine-level questions. Prior work applies steering either at every position or over a fixed prompt span, verifies success behaviourally, and treats the layer as a hyperparameter. The doctrine tested here adds timing (write only at high-entropy moments), coordinates (write through the band transport $J^{\top}u$ rather than a raw direction), verification (read the band back through a band-targeted lens rather than inspecting outputs alone), and an explicit intervention budget in nats. The negative result on contrastive refusal steering (Section~\ref{sec:results:l21}) does not contradict the contrastive literature, which reports effects on models and margins where refusal is more weakly consolidated \cite{Rimsky2024,Arditi2024}; it bounds what a single extracted direction achieves against an already-aligned 4B instruct model under this paper's registered criteria. The recognition-gated defence of Section~\ref{sec:results:moat} repurposes the same contrastive signal, extracted by the companion \emph{prayoga} attack library and injected through the doctrine's entropy-gated writer, as an input-conditioned gate rather than a blanket addition, which is what makes the difference between the negative and the deployable defence.

\subsection{Lenses and Readback}

The logit lens decodes intermediate residual states with the final unembedding \cite{Nostalgebraist2020}; the tuned lens fits per-layer affine probes to correct its distortions \cite{Belrose2023}. Sparse autoencoders and their successors decompose activations into dictionaries of interpretable features \cite{Bricken2023,Cunningham2023,Templeton2024}. The readback protocol used here differs in purpose: the lens is not an offline analysis tool but an online verification instrument inside a control loop, and the sensitivity advantage of band-targeted over final-target reading is quantified head-to-head against the Jacobian-lens baseline (Section~\ref{sec:results:baseline}). The band-articulation finding, that a lens targeted at the band recovers content a final-target lens misses at subtle doses, is one of the paper's replicated results (Sections~\ref{sec:results:articulation} and~\ref{sec:results:readback}); the benchmark also delimits it, since the two instruments converge once the write saturates.

\subsection{Active Inference for Experiment Selection}

Expected free energy balances epistemic value (expected information gain) against pragmatic value (expected preference satisfaction) and is the canonical action-selection quantity of active inference \cite{Friston2010,Friston2015,DaCosta2020,Parr2022}. The companion circuit-discovery work cast intervention selection inside a single interpretability experiment as a POMDP solved by expected-free-energy minimisation \cite{Sathish2026ACD}. The present programme lifts the same machinery one level: candidate experiments, not candidate interventions, form the action set; gate verdicts, not attribution observations, form the observation stream; and beliefs replay deterministically from the programme's own committed ledger. Section~\ref{sec:system} describes the resulting research-loop architecture and Appendix~\ref{app:efe} gives its parameters.

\subsection{Pratyabhij\~n\=a}

The recognition school (Pratyabhij\~n\=a) of Utpaladeva and Abhinavagupta holds that awareness is intrinsically self-aware and that this reflexivity is linguistic: \skt{vimar\'sa}, the mind's grasp of its own state, is identified with \skt{par\=a v\=ak}, the supreme Word (\emph{\={I}\'svarapratyabhij\~n\=ak\=arik\=a} 1.5.13) \cite{Torella2002}. The school's analysis distinguishes levels of speech from unarticulated to fully uttered, describes the flash of manifestation before a cognition stabilises (\skt{sphura\d{t}\d{t}\=a}) \cite{Dyczkowski1992}, treats received doctrine (\skt{\=agama}) as valid only when re-cognised by the receiving awareness, and catalogues the impairments (\skt{malas}) that restrict a subject's intrinsic freedom (\skt{sv\=atantrya}). Section~\ref{sec:doctrine} maps each element to a testable control-engineering clause. The mapping is instrumental: the philosophy contributes hypotheses precise enough to fail, and two of its suggested mechanisms did fail in their initial registered form (Sections~\ref{sec:results:timing} and~\ref{sec:results:l21}).
